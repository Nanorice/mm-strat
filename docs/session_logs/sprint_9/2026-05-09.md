## 💡 Context/Memory

- **The model is a top-K finder, not a full-universe ranker.** Using it with a continuous rank across all 4M rows produces confusing results (non-monotonic deciles). Its power is concentrated in the top 10% of scores, and the signal is strongly non-linear near the top.

- **Threshold is ~0.60, not 0.50.** The naive midpoint of the score range is not the right threshold. The empirical signal cliff sits at 0.435 (top decile entry) but meaningful edge only begins around 0.58–0.60 (sub-quintile 3). The ~0.65+ zone is where the model truly earns its keep.

- **Regime gate is NOT about improving signal quality in good regimes.** It prevents deployment on days when the signal actively inverts (Bear regime IC = −0.01). The gate improves the time-weighted effective IC from 0.059 → ~0.09 by sitting out bad periods.

- **breakout_ok IC = 0.67 is in-sample leakage.** The scores_cache was the training dataset. This number is informative for understanding the infra and signal structure, but must be validated OOS. The in-sample monotonicity (prob_elite increases with return_20d quintile, 0.18 → 0.50) shows the model has learned real structure.

- **Don't cap returns, drop bad tickers by name.** Extreme winners (ORGO 13x, KODK 12x) are the point of the model. Cap-based cleaning would destroy the signal. Maintain a `BAD_TICKERS` list with explicit reasons.

- **Score decays after breakout.** The trajectory shows score peaks at T=0 and bleeds back to ~0.40 over 30 days. This is the exit signal framework: monitor score daily during holding; exit when it drops below the decile 9 floor (~0.435).
