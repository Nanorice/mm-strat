## 1. Problem & Purpose

The SEPA scanner surfaces trade candidates (trend + breakout structure passes). On a busy day
it surfaces more than the book can hold. M01's job is the **ranking decision**: given today's
candidates, which are most likely to produce a large favorable move? It is a *selection* model —
it does not decide *whether* to be in the market (that's regime/timing), only *which names*
among those already qualified.

## 2. Theory / Hypothesis

Among names that already pass the SEPA structural filter, cross-sectional differences in
momentum, relative strength, volume dynamics, fundamentals, and regime context carry
information about *which* will run furthest. A gradient-boosted model on those features should
rank candidates by forward Max Favorable Excursion (MFE). The score is `prob_elite` =
P(MFE>30%) — the champion (`m01_binary`) predicts it directly (`binary:logistic`); the archived
4-class variant produced it as P(class Elite) of a 4-bin ordinal target (Dud/Noise/Solid/Elite,
cut at MFE 2/10/30%).

Regime (M03) is included deliberately: ablation shows it's a **net-positive mid-pack
contributor** (−0.22 Sharpe if dropped) — a *coincident state descriptor* ("given this regime,
which names work"), not a drawdown predictor. Good state descriptor ≠ good future predictor;
that's why M03 helps M01 selection but fails standalone as market timing.

## 2a. What the model actually is — the label/score-nature assessment (Sprint 14)

Sprint 14 spent weeks learning things about the *label and score* that were not obvious at design
time. This section is the honest read of the model's nature; treat it as load-bearing before
building anything on top of the score. Each claim names its **currency of proof** (C1 label-rank /
C2 OOS-rank / C3 exit-aware P&L — see memory `project_sepa_three_currencies`).

1. **The score is a GATE, not a within-pool ranker.** Inside the gated breakout pool the score's
   day-1 rank is ~uninformative on median outcome (**IC ≈ −0.03**); clipping to top-N-by-score
   *hurts* the median (all-survivors +4.3% > top-5 +3.2%). **The model earns its keep deciding
   *which* names are worth scoring, not their order.** Do not build a top-N-by-score product
   expecting rank skill in-pool — there is none. (`project_scoring_vs_selection_unclipped`.)

2. **The label is upside-only MFE — no downside twin.** The target measures *reachable* favorable
   excursion, not realized P&L. A high score means "this name can run," NOT "this name nets money
   after stops." A high-MFE name that gaps down through its stop is a loss the label never saw.

3. **The label is QUANTIZED — the tail is invisible at train time.** A classifier head bins +35%
   and +400% into the same "elite" bucket, so the score ranks *P(elite)*, never *expected
   magnitude*. `prob_elite` is discrete (~447 levels for binary — booster leaf-sum structure, not
   calibration). A magnitude-aware head was tried (M4) and tied the RS bar OOS — banked, not shipped.

4. **The score ranks the tail on the FULL universe (C1), but exits truncate it (C3 fails).** Pre-gate,
   on the full universe, the score does rank the fat right tail (home-run rate 0.2%→14.2% low→high
   decile, ρ_tail +0.19). That is a C1 win. It does **NOT** survive the 15% stop + trailing exit on
   the BackTrader cone (Q70 Stage-2 KILL) — the exits truncate the exact tail the gate concentrates.
   **This is the master lesson: label-lift ≠ trade-edge** (confirmed ~5× across RS-tail, minervini,
   hi-score). Every label-level metric on the model card is *advisory* for exactly this reason.

5. **Continuation-only, pro-cyclical, regime-blind — BY DESIGN.** SEPA screening (Stage-2 uptrend,
   rising MAs, high RS) structurally excludes crash-bottom reversals, so the model is a continuation
   model; it is pro-cyclical in crashes (2001/2008) as *scope*, not defect. The mean score is flat
   (0.37–0.45) through the entire 2022 bear while forward returns swing ±9% — **the score cannot see
   a drawdown coming.** Regime control must therefore be EXTERNAL (the SPY-200d deploy gate), not
   trained in. (`project_capital_deployment`.)

6. **Size is a real second, label-level axis — with an objective fork.** RS-D10 ∧ small-cap-T1 lifts
   home-run *rate* 2.4–3.2× (a C1 win). But mcap is monotone the *other* way on the *median* (mega
   +6.8% vs micro −3.8%). Small-cap tilt buys tail-odds at the cost of median — a genuine objective
   fork, not a bug; the shortlist deliberately tilts small (tail-odds objective). (`project_r1b_step2_subsumed`.)

> **Design consequence — keep the card and the gate separate:**
> - **The model card is STRATEGY-INDEPENDENT** (label-level, one card per model). It carries C1
>   metrics — AUC, calibration, IC, tail-lift@k, home-run-rate by score decile — plus threshold-free,
>   exit-free studies like the **equity fan** of daily candidates (raw separating power, no strategy
>   baked in). Every metric should carry a **currency label** (all C1 here) and a standing caveat that
>   label-level numbers overstate tradeable edge (label-lift ≠ trade-edge, §2a.4). The **cone does NOT
>   belong on the card** — it's a strategy verdict.
> - **The promotion GATE is strategy-aware** and lives elsewhere: the **C3 start-date cone** (§5),
>   never a label AUC or a single Sharpe. See `docs/architecture/model_development_methodology.md`
>   (8-gate pipeline; G6/G7 = the cone gate).

## 3. Specification

| Aspect | Value (champion `m01_binary`) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Algorithm** | XGBoost **binary classifier** (`binary:logistic`, `num_class=0`, `num_trees=100`) — verified from `models/m01_binary/v1/model.json` |
| **Target** | binary `home_run` = MFE > 30% (the "Elite" cut of `DEFAULT_MFE_BINS`, `src/evaluation/training_data_loader.py`). Score = `prob_elite` = P(MFE>30%). ⚠️ upside-only, quantized — see §2a. |
| **Feature set** | `fs_m01_prototype` — momentum/RS, volume, alphas, fundamentals, categoricals, 7× `m03_*` regime cols. Macro is **essential** for the binary target (no-macro halves the cone), unlike 4-class where it was redundant. |
| **Universe** | Sparse — SEPA trade candidates only (event-driven breakout rows, not the dense panel) |
| **Score scale** | `prob_elite` is **discrete (~447 levels)** — booster leaf-sum structure, NOT a calibrator artifact; gate by per-day rank/percentile, never an absolute floor (an absolute threshold is a cliff). |
| **Trees** | `num_trees=100` (binary → 1 tree/round). ⚠️ XGBoost does not persist `learning_rate`/`max_depth`/subsample/reg_* in `model.json`; treat any specific values as unverified. |
| **Preprocessing** | log-transform any `log_*` feature via `sign(x)·log(1+|x|)`; winsorize outliers |
| **Loadability** | ✅ has `categorical_mapping.json` → loads directly in the backtest scorer (unlike the archived 4-class, which needed `daily_predictions` injection). |

**Champion swap (Sprint 14, Q71):** binary replaced 4-class as prod after the start-date cone showed
it dominates (§5). The two produce the *same operation* — `prob_elite` = P(MFE>30%) — but binary's
score is threshold-cleaner and its cone is ~2× the alpha. Spearman(binary, 4-class) ≈ 0.96 but top-3/day
overlap only 70%, and their scales differ (0.163 vs 0.315) → always gate by per-model quantile, never an
absolute floor across models.

**Two-model system:** the selection champion (now `m01_binary`) = SELECTION, `m01_rank` = TIMING.
Complementary — do **not** test `m01_rank` as a standalone picker.

## 5. Performance

**The honest lens is the start-date CONE, not a single Sharpe.** A single backtest Sharpe is a
start-date lottery (folds swing >2 Sharpe on start-date alone); every promotion decision goes through
the distribution of per-cell Sharpe across rolling start dates, on **BackTrader** (the vectorized
engine is ~3× optimistic in bear folds — `project_vec_engine_optimistic`). Champion exit =
`champion_trail_spygate` (trend-exit-only trail + SPY-200d deploy gate).

Champion `m01_binary` vs archived 4-class, 89-cell cone (from `models/m01_binary/v1/evaluation/results.json`):

| Cone metric | m01_binary (prod) | m01_prototype 4-class (archived) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **median cell Sharpe** | **0.59** | 0.21 |
| floor (worst cell) | −1.73 | — |
| p25 / p75 | −0.11 / 1.10 | — |
| **% negative cells** | **30.3%** | 40.7% |
| median Calmar | 0.77 | 0.14 |
| **α annualized vs SPY** | **+15.6%** (β 0.58) | +7.3% |
| α vs QQQ | +13.9% (β 0.54) | — |

- **Binary carries ~2× the annualized alpha** at β≈0.58 — the single cleanest "binary is better" number,
  and it was invisible before the cone gate computed α/β (Sprint 14).
- The old "dead heat" (arena Sharpe ~2.0 both) was a **threshold-free-return artifact** on a short
  strong-tape window — binary's edge is threshold/risk-adjusted, so it only shows on the cone. The
  ~2.0 arena Sharpes were regime-flattered; do not cite them.
- **Promotion gate = the cone itself** (`aggregate_backtest_cone` in `walk_forward_backtest.py`:
  median/%neg/floor Sharpe + Calmar + α/β, incumbent-anchored). It replaced a stale 4-fold WF gate that
  was a 3-sample draw (Q71). Model card metrics are **label-level, advisory** — decoupled from this gate.
- Ablation artifact: `models/m01_binary/v1/evaluation/full_eval/ablation/ablation_summary.json`.

## 6. Version History / Journey

- **Sprints 1–12 — the selection ranker matured.** Converged on the 4-class MFE target
  (`DEFAULT_MFE_BINS`, ranked by `prob_elite`) and the `fs_m01_prototype` feature set;
  `m01_prototype_2003_2026/v1` became the production scorer feeding `daily_predictions`.
  (⚠️ earlier drafts of this doc described a `log_space`-MFE *regressor* — that was an experiment,
  not the shipped model; both v1 and v2 artifacts are `multi:softprob`. Corrected 2026-07-07.)
- **Two-model split — prototype (SELECTION) + rank (TIMING).** `m01_rank` added as the timing
  partner; the two are evaluated together, never rank alone.
- **Sprint 13 — strip-M03 experiment.** Built `fs_m01_no_macro`, retrained `m01_no_macro` and
  `m01_binary_no_macro`. **Result:** macro essential for binary, redundant for 4-class → keep M03
  in the champion; strip only where it's provably free.
- **Sprint 13 — honest backtest + arena.** Rebuilt the equity curve (bar-by-bar MtM), ran the
  all-variant arena and walk-forward gate. Looked like m01_binary ≈ m01_prototype (⟳ overturned by the
  Sprint 14 cone). See
  [2026-07-02_02_backtest_arena.md](../session_logs/sprint_13/logs/2026-07-02_02_backtest_arena.md).
- **Sprint 14 (2026-07-15) — BINARY PROMOTED to prod, 4-class ARCHIVED (Q71).** The "dead heat" was a
  threshold-free artifact: on the start-date **cone** binary dominates (median Sharpe 0.59 vs 0.21, ~2×
  alpha). The stale 4-fold promotion gate that blocked it was a biased 3-sample draw → **rebuilt as an
  incumbent-anchored cone gate**, not forced past (`project_binary_promoted_cone_gate`). `daily_predictions`
  backfilled with binary, dashboard + `v_d3_shortlist` serve it 0-NULL. Also: the whole sprint's
  label/score-nature findings (§2a) — gate-not-ranker, upside-only/quantized label, label-lift≠trade-edge,
  regime-blind-by-design. See RESEARCH_LOG Threads H–N, `project_4class_vs_binary`.

## 7. Usage

```bash
# Train (scan candidates → enrich features → train classifier → report)
.venv/Scripts/python.exe model_runner.py m01 --steps scan features train
# Full automated workflow (load → EDA → feature-select → train → report)
.venv/Scripts/python.exe model_runner.py workflow --start 2003-01-01 --end 2026-01-01
```

- **Prod scoring path:** M01 scores are materialized nightly into `daily_predictions` (never
  scored live in the dashboard). Prototype is scored via **`daily_predictions` injection** in the
  backtest because it lacks a frozen categorical vocab.
- **Backtest:** `scripts/run_backtest.py --model m01_prototype` (canonical path `score_from_t3()`).
  Only variants with `categorical_mapping.json` load directly (`m01_binary`, `*_no_macro`);
  `m01_prototype` **hard-fails** the scorer and must go through `daily_predictions`.

## 8. Roadmap / Open Questions

1. **Sync the ops box `sh019`** — the nightly scheduler there still scores 4-class until re-promoted/
   synced. Live-product gap, not research.
2. **Q69 — model-skill-regime gate (the one open research axis).** Do periods defined by the *model's
   own tail-capture skill* (not market trend) exist where a higher score gate pays? Needs a leak-free
   live-safe proxy for "the model is scoring well now." User also flagged: don't assume the axis is
   bull/bear, and put a question mark on whether the model is even good. Deferred, real.
3. **Magnitude-aware head (M4), reconsider only with a new angle.** A regressor/quantile head on
   `mfe_pct` tied the one-column RS bar OOS (banked, not shipped) — the classifier's tail-quantization
   (§2a.3) is real but a straight magnitude target didn't beat RS. Any revival must clear the C3 cone,
   not just a label metric.
4. **Retirement condition** — if the live forward **cone** decays (median cell Sharpe below the SEPA
   scanner's own base-rate edge over a quarter, or %neg-cells climbing past the incumbent), the ranking
   hypothesis is falsified for the live regime. Judge on the cone distribution, never a single Sharpe.

> **Saturated levers (do NOT re-propose — each has a kill on file):** within-pool re-ranking (IC≈−0.03),
> raising the score gate (variance knob, `project_prob_elite_gate_variance_knob`), a second regime axis
> beyond SPY-200d (5 falsifications, `project_regime_indicator_manual_null`), book-level brakes
> (governor / DD-breaker / earnings-blackout all rejected on the cone), wider/thinner book
> (Q46 wash), RS-tail de-gating (`project_sepa_three_currencies`).
