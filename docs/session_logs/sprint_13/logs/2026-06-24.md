## 💡 Context/Memory
- **THE headline finding:** *Nothing leads equity tails on this universe.* Tested 5 ways — level
  factors, refined credit (GZ ebp/est_prob), and cross-asset coupling (Absorption Ratio, the
  literature's best) — all coincident or worse. This is the most-replicated result, not an anomaly.
- **The anti-Kritzman discovery (Step 4b):** in the faithful AR×turbulence 2×2 grid, the **CALM
  quadrant has the WORST forward drawdowns** (−0.102). Crashes start from complacency, not visible
  coupling. Confirms §A2/§A3 ("danger is contrarian-bullish") at the cross-asset level.
- **Why the literature seemed to contradict us:** publication/in-sample bias (Kritzman's headline was
  a 2-event in-sample demo; the paper's own full-sample numbers concede ~40% false-positive + missed
  Covid) + window composition (our 2007+ window is 2/3 crises AR structurally can't catch). The
  literature over-generalized; our method was sound (we replicated their exact test and it failed).
- **Process discipline that worked:** every step was GATED on beating a simple baseline, and we
  *validated before believing* (the Step-3 walk-forward killed an in-sample 0.009 "win"). The whole
  point was to find the simplest model that works — and it turned out to be very simple (VIX + gate).
- **Decisions relaxed along the way:** design §1.1 explainability was formally relaxed (recorded in
  §10 as an override) — but the shipped model is explainable anyway, so it was moot.
