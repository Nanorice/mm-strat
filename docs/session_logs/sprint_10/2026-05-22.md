# Session Handover: 2026-05-22

## 🧭 High-Level Goal (anchor — do not deviate)
Two-model trading system. **m01_prototype** (prod, event-grain) is the
SELECTION model: "given breakout-day info on a SEPA-qualifying ticker, will this
trade reach good MFE before the trend breaks?" — works well, high prob → good
trade. **m01_rank** (this work, dense-grain) is the TIMING/EXECUTION model on top
of it: because a breakout typically pulls back before continuing, m01_rank
evaluates the setup DAILY to answer *when to enter / hold / rebalance / exit* on
a name m01_prototype already selected. m01_rank is NOT a standalone stock-picker.
(Open debt: m01_prototype's own dense-data backtest is unverified — confirm it.)

Audit whether `m01_rank`'s implausible backtest (28× return / Sharpe ~2) was a

## 💡 Context/Memory
- **The 28× was a stack of compounding measurement artifacts, not a real edge.** The most
  insidious: an all-NULL `adj_close` made the backtest book 0.0/day for every position,
  which looked like a *real negative-signal finding* (smooth cost-only NAV decay). Drew the
  wrong conclusion from it twice. Lesson saved to memory: assert return series is non-null
  with sane min/median/max BEFORE interpreting any backtest.
- **`t3_sepa_features` is dense over the screener-ACTIVE universe** (T3 uplift, already
  done) — NOT all-days. Measuring "gaps" against all price_data days falsely flags the
  legitimately-inactive days. t3 and t2 are both correct.
- **Two-engine disagreement is the diagnostic:** daily-rebalance positive + 20d-hold
  negative means the daily result harvests short-horizon drift correlated with high prob
  in up-markets, NOT the model's 20d prediction. The model's native-horizon claim doesn't pay.
- **`adr_20d` is genuine signal** (tight-range consolidation = the SEPA/VCP thesis), not a
  dense-grain artifact — confirmed by block-thinning going the opposite way to the artifact
  hypothesis.
