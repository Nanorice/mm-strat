### M03 Pillar Decomposition ✅
Tested each pillar as standalone gate:

| Gate | IC | Rows | Verdict |
|------|-----|------|---------|
| No gate | 0.065 | 100% | Baseline |
| **Trend pillar > 60** | **0.118** | 70% | Best single filter |
| **Trend + Liq > 60** | **0.126** | 38% | Best combo |

**Key findings**:
1. **Trend pillar does all the work** — use `m03_pillar_trend > 60` instead of `m03_score > 60`
2. **Liquidity pillar adds marginal value** — Trend + Liq (0.126) beats Trend alone (0.118)
3. **Risk pillar is useless/harmful** — VIX/HY thresholds are poorly calibrated
4. **Composite weighting (40/30/30) is suboptimal** — dilutes the trend signal

## 💡 Context/Memory

- **Trend pillar is the only M03 component that matters.** The risk pillar (VIX/HY) adds no predictive value — its fixed thresholds are poorly calibrated. The composite's 40/30/30 weighting dilutes the signal.

- **5-Factor and M03 are complementary, not competing.** The combined gate (IC=0.105) beats either alone (0.094–0.097). They capture different aspects of regime risk.

- **5F veto mechanism is broken.** "No veto" gate has *lower* IC than no gate at all. The 2σ threshold is too aggressive or the factors it monitors aren't predictive.

- **Liquidity pillar is unique.** It's the only M03 factor not already captured by 5F (VIX, HY, term spread, slope). Worth adding to 5F model.

- **Use medians, not means, for decile analysis.** A single bad ticker can move means by hundreds of percent. Medians are robust and show clean monotonicity.

- **BAD_TICKERS = {'LIF', 'CUE'}** — both have corrupted `return_1d` calculations. LIF: ticker reuse artifact. CUE: misaligned price join. Don't cap returns; drop by name only.
