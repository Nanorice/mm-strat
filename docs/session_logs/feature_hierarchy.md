# Quantamental Feature Hierarchy & Clustering

This document categorizes the 100+ features generated by the [FeaturePipeline](file:///c:/Users/Hang/PycharmProjects/quantamental/src/feature_pipeline.py#34-819) (found in [src/feature_pipeline.py](file:///c:/Users/Hang/PycharmProjects/quantamental/src/feature_pipeline.py)) into a logical hierarchy based on their underlying calculation logic. This classification will help us structure our EDA, identify redundant features, and understand the nature of the data before blindly feeding it into automated screeners.

## 1. Core Price & Volume (The Raw Data)
These are the base metrics directly derived from daily trading activity.

*   **Price Action:** `open`, `high`, `low`, `close`
*   **Volume Metrics:** `volume`, `turnover` (close * volume)
*   **Simple Deltas:** `return_1d`

## 2. Moving Averages & Trend Indicators
These features capture the smoothed direction of the price and volume over various time horizons.

*   **Simple Moving Averages (Price):** `sma_20`, `sma_50`, `sma_150`, `sma_200`, `sma_200_lag20`
*   **Distance from SMA:** `price_vs_sma_50`, `price_vs_sma_150`, `price_vs_sma_200`
*   **Moving Averages (Volume):** `vol_avg_20`, `vol_avg_50`, `dollar_volume_avg_20`
*   **Trend Slopes/Flags:** `sma_50_slope`, `close_above_sma200`, `trend_ok` (composite flag)

## 3. Momentum & Relative Strength (RS)
Features measuring how fast the price is moving relative to itself or a benchmark (SPY).

*   **Absolute Momentum (Returns):** `return_5d`, `return_20d`, `return_60d`
*   **Long-Term Momentum:** `mom_21d`, `mom_63d`, `mom_126d`, `mom_189d`, `mom_252d`
*   **Relative Strength vs SPY (Base):** `price_vs_spy`, `price_vs_spy_ma20`, `price_vs_spy_ma50`, `price_vs_spy_ma63`, `price_vs_spy_ma200`
*   **Relative Strength vs SPY (Derived):** `rs_line_log`, `rs_line_delta`, `rs_line_lag_delta`, `rs_line_uptrend`
*   **Composite RS Ratings:** `rs_rating` (weighted avg of long-term momentum), `rs` (alias), `rs_ma`
*   **Velocity/Acceleration:** `rs_velocity`, `price_momentum_curve`, `price_accel_10d`, `immediate_thrust`
*   **Cross-Sectional Ranks (Phase C):** `RS_Universe_Rank`, `RS_Sector_Rank`, `RS_vs_Sector`, `Sector_Momentum`, `RS_Industry_Rank`, `RS_vs_Industry`, `Industry_Momentum`

## 4. Volatility & Ranges
Features capturing the expansion and contraction of price action.

*   **Historical Highs/Lows:** `high_52w`, `low_52w`, `highest_high_20d`, `lowest_low_20d`, `high_20d`
*   **Distance to Extremes:** `dist_from_52w_high`, `dist_from_52w_low`, `dist_from_20d_low`, `dist_from_20d_high`, `pct_from_high_52w`, `pct_above_low_52w`
*   **True Range/ATR:** `atr_14`, `atr_20d`, `natr` (normalized ATR)
*   **Price Volatility:** `volatility_20d`, `adr_20d` (Average Daily Range)
*   **Consolidation Metrics (VCP):** `vcp_ratio` (atr_10 / atr_50), `consolidation_width`, `consolidation_duration`
*   **Volume Volatility:** `vol_ratio`, `vol_ratio_50`, `dry_up_volume`, `volume_acceleration`, `log_volume_velocity`

## 5. Technical Oscillators & Patterns
Specific technical setups or bounded indicators.

*   **RSI:** `rsi_14` (calculated manually from gains/losses)
*   **Day Types:** `is_green_day`, `green_days_ratio_20d`
*   **Breakout Detectors:** `breakout`, `breakout_momentum`, `breakout_ok` (composite flag)

## 6. Custom Alpha Factors (WorldQuant Style)
These are complex, mathematical, cross-sectional or time-series rank combinations calculated via Python in Phase B. They often involve non-linear transformations and interactions between price and volume.

*   [alpha001](file:///c:/Users/Hang/PycharmProjects/quantamental/src/feature_pipeline.py#476-487) to [alpha101](file:///c:/Users/Hang/PycharmProjects/quantamental/src/feature_pipeline.py#613-616) (16 factors total). Examples:
    *   [alpha006](file:///c:/Users/Hang/PycharmProjects/quantamental/src/feature_pipeline.py#505-510): `-1 * corr(open, volume, 10)`
    *   [alpha012](file:///c:/Users/Hang/PycharmProjects/quantamental/src/feature_pipeline.py#533-536): [sign(delta(vol,1)) * (-1 * delta(close,1))](file:///c:/Users/Hang/PycharmProjects/quantamental/src/evaluation/feature_screener.py#234-243)
    *   [alpha101](file:///c:/Users/Hang/PycharmProjects/quantamental/src/feature_pipeline.py#613-616): [(close - open) / ((high - low) + 0.001)](file:///c:/Users/Hang/PycharmProjects/quantamental/src/feature_pipeline.py#454-461)

## 7. Market Regime (M03)
Macro-level context features applied equally to all tickers on a given date (Phase D & E).

*   **Base Scores:** `m03_score`, `m03_pillar_trend`, `m03_pillar_liq`, `m03_pillar_risk`
*   **Derived Regime Metrics:** `m03_delta_5d`, `m03_delta_20d`, `m03_regime_vol`

## Suggested EDA Approach Based on Hierarchy

To avoid being overwhelmed by 100+ columns, we should analyze them in targeted groups:

1.  **Macro & Target Check:** Verify `target_col` against `m03_score` to understand if returns were strictly regime-driven.
2.  **Multicollinearity Triage:** 
    *   Plot correlation matrix just for the **Moving Averages** group. (e.g., `sma_50` and `sma_150` might be 0.99 correlated; the screener would randomly drop one, but we might want to keep the slope instead).
    *   Plot correlation matrix for **Momentum** metrics.
3.  **Fat-Tail Hunt in Volatility:** The **Volatility & Ranges** and **Volume Metrics** groups are the most likely to have extreme values (e.g., `volume_acceleration`, `turnover`). These should be prioritized for skewness/kurtosis checks.
4.  **Alpha Non-Linearity:** The **Custom Alpha Factors** are designed to be complex. A linear correlation (IC) will likely fail them. Hexbin or fractional/decile return plots are critical here.
