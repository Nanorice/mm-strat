## 💡 Context/Memory
- **The screenshot the user reviewed was the MOCK, not the built board.** Its Risk
  Regime prints "10/10 indicators" but **8 of those 10 rows are `null` in the mock's
  own data** (VVIX/SKEW/DXY/Gold/USD-JPY/BTC/futures = C3, deferred). Mock density is
  partly fabricated — do not treat it as a coverage target.
- **The proposed 0.5σ/1.0σ banner thresholds were REJECTED on measurement**: over 120
  days, 0.5σ fires on a median **14 of 56 rows EVERY day**, 1.0σ on 6. Not tuning —
  |z|≥1 is ~32% of a normal distribution, so ~18 of 56 fire on an ordinary day.
  Shipped 1.5/2.5 (median 2 amber / 0 red); test-guarded against silent lowering.
- **Two statistical traps in the z, both caught by measuring:** (1) absolute change is
  NOT stationary for a trending price — gold's full-history σ is $22.69 but it was
  $350 in 2003 vs $4,000 now, so a routine 1% day scored 1.8σ (first cut: 21 red/31
  amber); (2) pct-change fixes that (gold σ 1.146% full vs 1.154% since 2021) but
  **explodes on a zero-crossing** — T10Y2Y scored a 22.6% σ. Hence per-unit choice:
  **pct for prices, absolute for spreads** (`S3_PCT_UNITS`, keyed off the existing
  `unit` field — no new flag).
- **Yahoo BEATS FRED for commodities** — deeper (2003+), daily, and it has **cocoa**
  (FRED has no clean series; no US ETF either). Yahoo `CL=F` is even fresher than FRED
  `DCOILWTICO` (T+0 vs T+2) so it owns the WTI row. Prefer Yahoo for future price
  series. Uranium = URA ETF (2010+); no futures contract exists.
- 🐛 **I reported "remote parity holds ✓" and it was WRONG.** The check read the MAIN
  db through the loader (`_connect()` fell back), reporting main's 49 symbols as if
  they were the slim DB's — which actually had 12. **The tell was in my own output: an
  identical row count between a full DB and a *windowed* slim DB is implausible.**
  Verify parity by opening `dashboard.duckdb` **directly**, and always rebuild AFTER
  an ingest. Now recorded in `[[project_dashboard_remote_parity]]`.
- **Smoke-test row COUNTS, not just HTTP 200**: all 36 FRED IDs returned 200, but
  `BAMLC0A0CM` (IG OAS) is **785 rows from 2023-07** — not deep like its HY sibling
  `BAMLH0A0HYM2` (6,148, 2003+); it would fail M03's 10yr-z bar like the sprint-13
  `MOVE` rejection. `EXHOSLUSM495S` is re-based → 13 rows.
- **22 series are REVISED-at-first-print** (`INSERT OR IGNORE` → first write wins,
  revisions dropped). Fine for a display board; **display-only**, never a model input.
  Upgrade path = `INSERT OR REPLACE` (cf `cape_engine`). The z also uses all-time
  moments = **look-ahead** → same display-only posture as the 6 pillars / CAPE_OURS.
- **No per-row histogram of daily %Δ** (user floated it): a histogram needs ~50+ points
  for a shape, but 22 of the 42 FRED series are monthly/quarterly (6 points in a 180d

  unusual?" question in one number. Same failure mode as the original 180-points-in-64px
  sparkline: **don't draw more resolution than the data supports.**
