# Q46 — capacity arm (10 slots @ 10%): Sharpe holds at 2× breadth — capacity fact, not a promotion

**Date**: 2026-07-11 · **Thread**: J · **Arm**: `champion_trail_spygate_n10` (registry, ONE
ex-ante arm — no sweep, cone-fitting guard)
**Design**: champion_trail_spygate with the book doubled: 10 slots @ 10% sizing (same pool,
score floor, trail exit, SPY-200d gate). Tests the Q44 prediction that the near-book-quality
`no_slots` queue can absorb more capital without collapsing the edge. Distinct from the closed
Q14 (same-day top-10 widening): this deepens the book ACROSS days.

## Reading

1. **The Q44 queue-quality prediction is CONFIRMED**: doubling breadth (drawing 2× the names,
   reaching rank ~6–10 of the daily queue across days) holds the median Sharpe flat (+0.05 ≈
   noise). The edge does not decay at 2× capacity — the marginal name is as good as the booked
   name, in realized P&L, not just labels.
2. **But it is NOT a better strategy**: the mid-left tail worsens (p25 −0.29) and dispersion
   widens (IQR +0.42) — smaller positions in more names did not smooth the cone; the extra
   names add regime-correlated exposure, not diversification (name-luck was never the binding
   risk; regime-luck is, and 10 names share the regime same as 5).
3. **Practical meaning**: at the ~$7.5M/day liquidity constraint, halving position size doubles
   deployable capital at ~unchanged expectancy. Banked as the CAPACITY envelope of the champion,
   not as a config change.

## Verdict

**Champion stays at 5 slots.** n10 is banked as a capacity fact: the strategy supports ~2×
capital via breadth at flat median Sharpe, with slightly fatter mid-tail dispersion. Together
with Q45 (cadence is a wash), the portfolio-layer conclusion is: **the champion's book design is
not leaving Sharpe on the table — its constraint is regime concentration, which no portfolio
knob tested (cadence, breadth, stops, gates beyond SPY-200d) removes.**

## Caveats
- $25k initial cash → $2.5k positions in n10; commission/slippage (0.1%/0.1%) scale-invariant
  here, but real-world minimums would differ at small capital.
- One arm, one slot count (10) — deliberately un-swept; the claim is "capacity ~2× at flat
  Sharpe", not an optimal-N statement.
